About

Hey, bud! I'm Andrew, a designer from Philly.

I like to make things that have both physical and digital dimensions to them. I have a degree in Industrial Design from The University of the Arts. While I was there, I also took a lot of Multimedia classes, which gave me a passion for technology and programming. I like to converge these two feilds in the form of interactive installations or wearable devices.

I'm always looking at our relationships to technology and the ways we wield it: how it does things for us, and to us.

When I'm not busy making things, I enjoy exploring the city, sharing cheesy puns with friends, finding cool/inspiring videos on YouTube/Vimeo, or trying to develop a new skill like musicianship, knitting, or meditation.

Sometimes, I also do collaborations at the Philly Game Forge and make fun game prototypes like this.



Some Designers and Professionals that inspire me:

Dunne & Raby: A design duo from the UK that make provocative, discursive designs that question the social, cultural, and ethical implications of technological innovations.

Syd Mead: A futurist concept artist that can envision settings of possible futures that feel warm, attainable, and lived-in.

Daniel Shiffman: A professor at ITP that evangelizes Processing, a designerly programing language adapted from java.

Roman Mars: On his podcast, 99% Invisible, Roman investigates the aspects of design just below the surface of our everyday lives. Things in plain sight, and yet completely subconscious to us.
